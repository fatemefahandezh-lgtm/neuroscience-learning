## 3. What Is Actually Inherited?

- **Genotype**: genetic constitution  
- **Phenotype**: observable traits (including behavioral tendencies)  
- **Alleles**: different versions of a gene  
- **Homozygous**: two identical alleles  
- **Heterozygous**: two different alleles  

Behavior reflects the interaction between inherited alleles and environmental experience.

## 5. Monogenic vs Polygenic Traits

### Monogenic Traits
- Caused by mutation in one gene
- High penetrance
- Clear inheritance pattern

### Polygenic Traits
- Influenced by many genes
- Each gene contributes small effect
- Strong environmental modulation

Most psychiatric and behavioral traits are **polygenic or oligogenic**.

## 2-Minute Oral Summary

Genes do not encode behaviors directly; they encode proteins that shape neural circuits. Behavior is the output of those circuits interacting with environmental inputs. While some disorders result from single-gene mutations, most behavioral traits are polygenic and influenced by many genes of small effect. Twin studies show heritability, but heritability does not imply determinism. Ultimately, behavior reflects continuous interaction between genetic predispositions and environmental experience.
